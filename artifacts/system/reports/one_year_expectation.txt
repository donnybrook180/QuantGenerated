One-Year Live Account Expectation
Date: 2026-04-10
Account size: EUR 100,000

Purpose
This is a practical scenario note for the current live stack after:
- risk-based live sizing
- and removing USDJPY, US100, and US500 from live trading

It is not a promise.

Current live universe
- BTC
- ETH
- EURUSD
- GBPUSD
- JP225
- XAUUSD

Current live sizing assumption
- Base risk per trade is 1.5% of account equity when effective strategy weight = 1.0.
- At effective strategy weight 0.5, risk per trade is 0.75%.
- MT5 order size is derived from:
  - account equity
  - strategy effective weight
  - ATR-based stop distance

What changed versus the previous estimate
The previous estimate still included three symbols you did not trust:
- USDJPY
- US100
- US500

Those are now out of live.

This improves the live book in two ways:
- less weak or long-underwater exposure
- lower correlation and lower chance that fragile reduced-risk sets still drag the account

Current deployment quality snapshot
Stronger / cleaner:
- EURUSD
- XAUUSD
- BTC
- ETH

Still more cautious:
- GBPUSD
- JP225

Interpretation
The live book is now smaller, but cleaner.
That should reduce some downside tail risk from weak symbols, while still leaving enough activity through forex, metals, and crypto.

The trade-off is:
- less diversification by count
- but better average quality per live symbol

Evidence used
1. Portfolio forward allocator backtest
   Source: artifacts/system/reports/portfolio_allocator_forward_backtest.txt
   Historical forward evidence is still essentially flat, so confidence remains limited.

2. Fill-aware allocator backtest
   Source: artifacts/system/reports/portfolio_allocator_fill_backtest.txt
   Still no meaningful MT5 fill history for the current live configuration.

3. Current deployment quality
   The weakest discretionary exclusions are now removed from the live book.

Scenario estimate for 1 year
Pessimistic but realistic:
- Ending equity: EUR 65,000 to EUR 88,000
- Meaning: even the cleaner live set underperforms, and 1.5% sizing still makes drawdowns meaningful.

Base case:
- Ending equity: EUR 90,000 to EUR 125,000
- Meaning: stronger symbols do enough to keep the book around flat to moderately profitable, while the remaining weaker names stay contained.

Optimistic but still defensible:
- Ending equity: EUR 130,000 to EUR 175,000
- Meaning: EURUSD, XAUUSD, BTC, and ETH carry the account, and the smaller remaining names do not materially hurt.

Point estimate
- EUR 108,000

Why this is better than the previous estimate
- You removed symbols you already judged as not good enough.
- That is usually better than hoping sizing or reduced-risk logic will save weak edges.

Why this is still not a high-confidence bullish forecast
- The live sizing is still meaningful at 1.5% risk per full-weight trade.
- Fill-based truth is still missing.
- GBPUSD and JP225 are not in the same quality tier as EURUSD or XAUUSD.

What would move this estimate up
- Real MT5 fills show that EURUSD, XAUUSD, BTC, and ETH hold up in practice.
- GBPUSD and JP225 remain small contributors or improve.
- Slippage remains contained.

What would move this estimate down
- Crypto contributes more volatility than expected.
- Live execution quality is worse than research assumes.
- The smaller live universe turns out to be more correlated than expected in stress periods.

Bottom line
Removing USDJPY, US100, and US500 improves the quality of the live book.
It does not remove risk, but it does make the one-year outlook cleaner:
- still real downside
- still no proof yet
- but a slightly better expected outcome than before
