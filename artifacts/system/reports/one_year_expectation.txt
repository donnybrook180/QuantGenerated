One-Year Live Account Expectation
Date: 2026-04-09
Account size: EUR 100,000

Purpose
This is a practical scenario note for the current live stack after the switch from symbol-level portfolio budgeting to agent-first live sizing.
It is not a promise.

Current live universe
- BTC
- ETH
- EURUSD
- GBPUSD
- JP225
- US100
- US500
- USDJPY
- XAUUSD

Important change versus the previous estimate
The previous estimate assumed symbol-level portfolio weights that heavily diluted position sizes as more symbols were added.
That is no longer how live sizing works.

Current live sizing logic
- Each live strategy now effectively starts at full portfolio weight in the live loop.
- Reduced-risk behavior now comes mainly from the strategy itself:
  - base_allocation_weight
  - max_risk_multiplier
  - regime gating
  - symbol-level side allocation when multiple strategies compete inside one symbol

What this means
- More symbols no longer automatically make every other symbol tiny.
- Activity and gross exposure can now be materially higher than in the old estimate.
- This raises both upside and downside.

Key evidence used
1. Portfolio forward allocator backtest
   Source: artifacts/system/reports/portfolio_allocator_forward_backtest.txt
   The historical forward allocator test is still essentially flat.
   However, it is now less representative because live is no longer using symbol-level portfolio dilution in the same way.

2. Fill-aware allocator backtest
   Source: artifacts/system/reports/portfolio_allocator_fill_backtest.txt
   Still no MT5 fills were available, so there is still no hard broker-executed performance history for the current live stack.

3. Current deployment quality
   Stronger / broader:
   - USDJPY
   - BTC
   - ETH
   - EURUSD
   - XAUUSD

   Narrow / fragile / reduced-risk:
   - US100
   - US500
   - GBPUSD
   - JP225

Interpretation
The system is now closer to the intended design:
- treat strategies as separate opportunities
- only reduce risk when the specific strategy or regime calls for it

That is a better match for your stated goal.
But it also means the old near-flat estimate is too conservative on activity, and too narrow on risk.

Scenario estimate for 1 year
Pessimistic but realistic:
- Ending equity: EUR 70,000 to EUR 90,000
- Meaning: higher activity reveals that some current reduced-risk or fragile deployments still have weak equity paths after costs and slippage.

Base case:
- Ending equity: EUR 90,000 to EUR 115,000
- Meaning: stronger symbols contribute enough to keep the book around flat to moderately positive, but weaker sets still create drag.

Optimistic but still defensible:
- Ending equity: EUR 120,000 to EUR 145,000
- Meaning: the stronger regime-aware strategies carry the book, reduced-risk strategies stay contained, and live execution quality is decent.

Point estimate
- EUR 106,000

Why the range is now wider
- The bot can actually do more now; it is no longer being flattened by symbol-level portfolio weights.
- That increases the chance of both meaningful gains and meaningful losses.
- There is still no fill-based proof for the current live sizing regime.

What would make this estimate move up materially
- Real MT5 fills accumulate and the fill-aware backtest becomes clearly positive.
- USDJPY, BTC, ETH, EURUSD, and XAUUSD continue to show clean realized live PnL.
- Reduced-risk symbols either improve or stay small enough not to drag the book.

What would make this estimate move down
- Execution/slippage is materially worse than research assumes.
- Reduced-risk symbols still fire often enough to hurt but not often enough to help.
- Correlated strategies across symbols all underperform in the same period.

Bottom line
Under the new live sizing logic, expecting "almost nothing happens" is no longer the right model.
The current best estimate is now:
- not a guaranteed strong profit
- but a real chance of moderate upside, with clearly higher downside risk than the old estimate implied.
